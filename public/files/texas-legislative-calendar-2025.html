89th Regular Legislative Session

January 14, 2025 - May 26, 2025

Introduction

The 89th Texas Legislature convenes on January 14, 2025, for its regular 140-day session, concluding on May 26, 2025. This calendar provides a comprehensive overview of key dates, deadlines, and events during the legislative session to help organizations and individuals effectively plan their legislative engagement strategies.

The Texas Legislature meets in regular session every two years, making this concentrated period critical for advancing policy priorities. Understanding the legislative timeline is essential for effective advocacy, as specific deadlines determine when bills must be filed, heard in committee, and passed by each chamber.

This calendar highlights:

  • Constitutional deadlines for legislative action
  • Key procedural dates in both the House and Senate
  • Committee hearing schedules and deadlines
  • Recess periods and state holidays
  • Strategic planning considerations for advocates

Use this calendar to develop a structured approach to legislative engagement, ensuring that advocacy efforts align with the procedural rhythm of the session. As each legislative session has unique dynamics, these dates serve as guideposts within the broader context of the legislative process.

Strategic Planning Considerations

The 24-Month Cycle Strategy

Success in the 140-day regular legislative session is largely determined by work done during the 18-month interim period before the session begins. Organizations that approach legislative advocacy as a continuous, two-year process consistently outperform those that engage only when sessions begin.

Critical Pre-Session Activities

  • Interim Committee Engagement: Participate in interim committee hearings addressing your priority issues
  • Relationship Development: Build and strengthen relationships with legislators and staff without immediate legislative demands
  • Issue Development: Refine policy proposals based on stakeholder input and technical analysis
  • Coalition Building: Establish formal or informal coalitions around shared policy objectives
  • Legislative Champion Recruitment: Identify and secure commitment from potential authors and sponsors

In-Session Strategic Phases

The 140-day session naturally divides into distinct strategic phases, each requiring different approaches:

  1. Organizational Phase (Days 1-30): Focus on relationship-building with newly appointed committee chairs and members
  2. Committee Phase (Days 31-90): Concentrate efforts on securing hearings and favorable committee action
  3. Calendar Management Phase (Days 91-120): Navigate the complex calendaring process to ensure floor consideration
  4. Endgame Phase (Days 121-140): Manage amendments, conference committees, and final passage negotiations

Post-Session Engagement

The conclusion of the regular session marks the beginning of important implementation work:

  • Executive Action Monitoring: Track gubernatorial action on passed legislation
  • Implementation Planning: Engage with agencies as they develop rules and procedures
  • Interim Issue Development: Begin preparing for the next legislative cycle
  • Relationship Maintenance: Continue stakeholder and legislator engagement during the interim
