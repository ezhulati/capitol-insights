Telecommunications Regulatory Outlook for Texas

An overview of upcoming regulatory changes affecting the telecommunications industry in Texas and strategies for adaptation.

July 18, 2024
Byron Campbell, Senior Partner
Telecommunications

Executive Summary

The telecommunications regulatory landscape in Texas is undergoing significant transformation, driven by technological innovation, federal policy shifts, and evolving state priorities. This briefing provides a methodical analysis of current and upcoming regulatory changes affecting the telecommunications industry in Texas and offers strategic recommendations for stakeholders to navigate this dynamic environment.

Our analysis indicates that while regulatory changes present compliance challenges, they also create opportunities for industry participants who can adapt strategically. The most successful organizations will be those that take a proactive approach to regulatory engagement while developing flexible business models that can accommodate evolving requirements.

Key findings from this briefing include:

  • Federal broadband funding programs will significantly impact Texas telecommunications infrastructure development
  • State-level implementation of federal programs requires careful navigation of overlapping jurisdictions
  • Emerging technologies like 5G and satellite broadband face evolving regulatory frameworks
  • Rural and underserved area connectivity remains a regulatory priority at both state and federal levels
  • Data privacy and security regulations continue to evolve with implications for telecommunications providers

Current Regulatory Landscape

The telecommunications industry in Texas operates within a complex regulatory framework involving federal, state, and local authorities. Understanding this landscape is essential for effective strategic planning.

Key Regulatory Authorities

  • Federal Communications Commission (FCC): Primary federal regulator overseeing interstate and international communications
  • Public Utility Commission of Texas (PUCT): State regulator with authority over intrastate telecommunications services
  • Texas Broadband Development Office (BDO): Recently established state office responsible for broadband expansion initiatives
  • Texas Legislature: Establishes statutory framework for telecommunications regulation in Texas
  • Local Governments: Manage rights-of-way, permitting, and certain aspects of infrastructure deployment

Recent Regulatory Developments

Several significant regulatory developments have occurred in the past year:

  • Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA): Allocated approximately $42.5 billion nationally for broadband deployment through the Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment (BEAD) Program, with Texas expected to receive over $3.3 billion
  • Texas Broadband Plan: Released by the Broadband Development Office in June 2023, establishing the state's strategy for broadband expansion
  • Digital Opportunity Data Collection (DODC): New FCC broadband mapping initiative providing more granular data on service availability
  • Open RAN Development: Increasing regulatory focus on open radio access network technologies to promote competition and security
  • Universal Service Fund Reform: Ongoing discussions at both federal and state levels regarding the sustainability and structure of universal service mechanisms

Regulatory Jurisdiction and Tensions

The telecommunications regulatory environment is characterized by overlapping jurisdictions and occasional tensions:

  • Federal vs. State Authority: Ongoing debates over the boundaries between federal and state regulatory jurisdiction
  • State vs. Local Control: Tensions between state-level standardization and local authority over infrastructure deployment
  • Technology Neutrality: Questions about whether regulations should be technology-specific or technology-neutral
  • Legacy vs. Emerging Services: Regulatory frameworks designed for traditional telecommunications services being applied to new technologies and business models

Key Regulatory Focus Areas

Based on our analysis of regulatory trends and stakeholder discussions, we anticipate several key focus areas for telecommunications regulation in Texas over the next 12-24 months:

Broadband Infrastructure Expansion

Broadband deployment remains a top regulatory priority:

  • BEAD Program Implementation: The Texas Broadband Development Office is developing the state's implementation plan for allocating over $3.3 billion in federal funding
  • Unserved/Underserved Area Prioritization: Regulatory frameworks increasingly focus on directing resources to areas lacking adequate broadband service
  • Middle-Mile Infrastructure: Growing recognition of the importance of middle-mile networks in supporting last-mile connectivity
  • Technology Neutrality Debates: Ongoing discussions about whether funding should be technology-neutral or favor specific deployment models
  • Sustainability Requirements: Increasing focus on ensuring long-term sustainability of subsidized networks

5G and Advanced Wireless Deployment

Next-generation wireless technologies face evolving regulatory frameworks:

  • Small Cell Deployment: Continued implementation of streamlined permitting processes for small cell infrastructure
  • Spectrum Allocation: FCC actions on mid-band spectrum with implications for Texas deployments
  • Local Zoning Authority: Ongoing tensions between federal/state streamlining and local control
  • RF Exposure Standards: Potential updates to radiofrequency exposure guidelines and compliance requirements
  • Infrastructure Sharing Requirements: Evolving rules regarding infrastructure sharing and access

Universal Service Reform

Universal service mechanisms are undergoing significant changes:

  • Texas Universal Service Fund (TUSF): Ongoing discussions about the future structure and funding of the TUSF
  • Federal USF Contribution Reform: Potential changes to the federal USF contribution methodology
  • Program Consolidation: Efforts to streamline and consolidate various universal service programs
  • Affordability Initiatives: Expanded focus on service affordability alongside availability
  • Lifeline/ACP Evolution: Changes to federal low-income support programs with state implementation implications

Network Security and Resilience

Security and resilience requirements continue to evolve:

  • Supply Chain Security: Implementation of the Secure and Trusted Communications Networks Act requirements
  • Critical Infrastructure Designation: Implications of critical infrastructure designations for telecommunications providers
  • Cybersecurity Standards: Evolving cybersecurity requirements and reporting obligations
  • Disaster Preparedness: Enhanced requirements for network resilience and disaster recovery
  • Open RAN Development: Regulatory initiatives to promote open and interoperable network architectures

Consumer Protection and Service Quality

Consumer-focused regulations are expanding:

  • Broadband "Nutrition Labels": Implementation of standardized disclosure requirements for broadband services
  • Service Quality Standards: Potential development of broadband service quality metrics and reporting
  • Digital Discrimination: Implementation of FCC rules prohibiting digital discrimination
  • Privacy Protections: Evolving requirements for customer data protection and privacy
  • Billing Transparency: Enhanced requirements for clear and transparent billing practices

The BEAD Program Opportunity

The Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment (BEAD) Program represents an unprecedented opportunity for telecommunications providers in Texas. With over $3.3 billion allocated to the state, this program will significantly reshape the competitive landscape. However, participation will require careful navigation of complex eligibility requirements, matching fund obligations, and long-term service commitments. Providers should begin preparing now by identifying potential service areas, developing deployment plans, and engaging with the Texas Broadband Development Office.

Strategic Implications for Industry Stakeholders

The evolving regulatory landscape has significant strategic implications for different types of telecommunications stakeholders in Texas:

Incumbent Providers

  • Regulatory Compliance Costs: Prepare for increased compliance costs associated with new reporting, service quality, and consumer protection requirements
  • Infrastructure Investment Strategies: Align capital investment plans with areas likely to receive funding support
  • Legacy Network Transition: Develop strategies for transitioning from legacy networks to next-generation technologies while managing regulatory obligations
  • Competitive Positioning: Assess how new funding programs may alter competitive dynamics in currently served markets
  • Regulatory Advocacy: Engage in the implementation process for new programs to shape requirements and obligations

Competitive Providers

  • Funding Opportunity Assessment: Evaluate eligibility for BEAD and other funding programs to support network expansion
  • Infrastructure Access: Leverage pole attachment and rights-of-way reforms to accelerate deployment
  • Partnership Strategies: Consider strategic partnerships to meet scale requirements for funding programs
  • Technology Selection: Assess how technology choices may impact funding eligibility and long-term compliance obligations
  • Market Entry Planning: Identify underserved markets that may become economically viable with subsidy support

Wireless Providers

  • Small Cell Deployment: Develop strategies to leverage streamlined small cell deployment processes
  • Spectrum Strategy: Position for upcoming spectrum allocations and potential secondary market opportunities
  • Fixed Wireless Opportunity: Assess opportunities to leverage fixed wireless for BEAD-funded deployments
  • Infrastructure Sharing: Prepare for potential new infrastructure sharing requirements and opportunities
  • Security Compliance: Develop comprehensive plans for meeting evolving network security requirements

Emerging Technology Providers

  • Regulatory Classification: Monitor evolving regulatory classifications for new technologies and service models
  • Technology Neutrality Advocacy: Engage in advocacy to ensure funding program technology neutrality
  • Performance Validation: Develop strategies to validate technology performance against evolving standards
  • Pilot Programs: Consider participation in regulatory pilot programs to demonstrate new technologies
  • Standards Development: Engage in technical standards development processes that may influence future regulations

Regulatory Engagement Strategies

Effective engagement with the regulatory process is essential for telecommunications stakeholders in this dynamic environment. We recommend the following approaches:

1. Develop a Comprehensive Regulatory Monitoring System

  • Multi-level Tracking: Establish systems to monitor regulatory developments at federal, state, and local levels
  • Early Identification: Identify emerging issues early in the regulatory process when influence opportunities are greatest
  • Impact Assessment: Develop methodologies to quickly assess potential business impacts of proposed regulations
  • Cross-functional Coordination: Ensure coordination between regulatory, legal, technical, and business teams
  • Competitive Intelligence: Monitor competitor positions and engagement in regulatory proceedings

2. Prioritize Strategic Regulatory Engagement

  • Selective Participation: Focus engagement resources on proceedings with the greatest potential business impact
  • Data-Driven Advocacy: Support regulatory positions with robust data and economic analysis
  • Coalition Building: Identify and collaborate with aligned stakeholders on shared regulatory priorities
  • Relationship Development: Cultivate relationships with key regulatory staff and decision-makers
  • Constructive Approach: Offer constructive alternatives rather than simply opposing problematic proposals

3. Align Business Strategy with Regulatory Trends

  • Regulatory Scenario Planning: Incorporate multiple regulatory scenarios into business planning processes
  • Flexible Deployment Models: Develop network deployment approaches that can adapt to evolving requirements
  • Compliance by Design: Integrate regulatory compliance considerations into product and service development
  • Strategic Positioning: Position business initiatives to align with regulatory priorities where possible
  • Regulatory Risk Management: Develop strategies to mitigate potential adverse regulatory outcomes

4. Leverage Funding and Incentive Programs

  • Program Expertise Development: Build internal expertise on complex funding program requirements
  • Application Preparation: Begin preparing key elements for funding applications in advance of deadlines
  • Compliance Planning: Develop comprehensive plans for meeting post-award obligations
  • Matching Fund Strategies: Identify sources for required matching funds where applicable
  • Program Engagement: Participate in program development processes to shape implementation details

5. Enhance Stakeholder Communication

  • Transparent Disclosure: Develop clear communication strategies for new regulatory requirements
  • Customer Education: Proactively educate customers about regulatory changes affecting services
  • Community Engagement: Engage with communities regarding infrastructure deployment plans
  • Investor Communication: Clearly articulate regulatory risks and opportunities to investors
  • Internal Alignment: Ensure internal stakeholders understand regulatory constraints and opportunities

Key Legislative Considerations

Several factors will influence telecommunications policy development in the 89th Legislative Session:

  • Federal-State Coordination: Need for alignment between state policies and federal funding program requirements
  • Rural-Urban Balance: Balancing the needs of rural and urban areas in infrastructure development policies
  • Technology Evolution: Ensuring regulatory frameworks can accommodate rapidly evolving technologies
  • Fiscal Constraints: Balancing funding needs with broader state budget priorities
  • Stakeholder Alignment: Degree of consensus among industry, consumer, and government stakeholders

Conclusion

The telecommunications regulatory landscape in Texas is undergoing significant transformation, presenting both challenges and opportunities for industry stakeholders. Federal broadband funding programs, evolving state policies, and technological innovation are driving regulatory changes that will reshape the competitive environment.

By taking a methodical and transparent approach to regulatory engagement, telecommunications stakeholders can effectively navigate this dynamic environment. This requires developing comprehensive regulatory monitoring systems, prioritizing strategic engagement, aligning business strategies with regulatory trends, leveraging funding programs, and enhancing stakeholder communication.

The 89th Texas Legislative Session will provide important opportunities to shape telecommunications policy for the coming years. Organizations that prepare now by developing clear policy positions, building stakeholder coalitions, and engaging with policymakers will be best positioned to influence outcomes and adapt to the evolving regulatory landscape.
