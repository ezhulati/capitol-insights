Municipal Advocacy Strategies for the 89th Legislative Session

Strategic guidance for municipalities seeking to effectively advocate for their interests in the upcoming legislative session.

August 5, 2024
Drew Campbell, President
Municipal Affairs

Executive Summary

The 89th Texas Legislative Session presents both significant challenges and opportunities for municipalities across the state. This briefing provides a straightforward assessment of the legislative landscape and offers practical strategies for effective municipal advocacy.

Our analysis indicates that municipalities will face continued pressure on local authority while navigating complex policy issues including property tax limitations, infrastructure funding, and public safety concerns. The most successful municipal advocacy efforts will be those that build genuine relationships with legislators, develop clear and compelling narratives, and leverage strategic coalitions.

Key findings from this briefing include:

  • Local control will remain a central tension in the 89th Legislative Session
  • Effective advocacy requires early engagement and relationship building
  • Data-driven approaches strengthen municipal policy positions
  • Regional coalitions can amplify municipal voices
  • Proactive communication strategies are essential for shaping the narrative

Legislative Landscape for the 89th Session

The 89th Texas Legislative Session will convene in January 2025 against a backdrop of continued economic growth, demographic shifts, and evolving policy priorities. Understanding this landscape is essential for developing effective municipal advocacy strategies.

Political Environment

Several factors will shape the political environment for the upcoming session:

  • Leadership Dynamics: Relationships between the Governor, Lieutenant Governor, and Speaker of the House will influence legislative priorities and the flow of legislation
  • Committee Assignments: Changes in committee leadership and membership will affect how municipal issues are considered
  • Urban-Rural Divide: Tensions between urban and rural interests continue to influence policy discussions
  • Election Impacts: The 2024 elections may shift the balance of power and policy priorities

Key Policy Areas for Municipalities

Based on interim committee hearings, stakeholder discussions, and historical trends, we anticipate several policy areas will be particularly significant for municipalities:

  • Local Revenue and Taxation: Continued pressure on property tax caps and potential restrictions on other revenue sources
  • Infrastructure Funding: Transportation, water, and broadband infrastructure needs amid competing budget priorities
  • Land Use and Development: Potential limitations on municipal zoning authority and development regulations
  • Public Safety: Debates over law enforcement funding, policies, and practices
  • Economic Development: Scrutiny of municipal economic development incentives and programs
  • Environmental Regulations: Tensions between state preemption and local environmental ordinances

Recent Legislative Trends

Several trends from recent sessions are likely to continue:

  • Preemption: Increasing state preemption of municipal authority across various policy areas
  • Unfunded Mandates: New requirements for municipalities without corresponding funding
  • Revenue Constraints: Continued limitations on municipal revenue-raising capacity
  • Accelerated Timelines: Fast-moving legislation requiring rapid municipal response
  • Polarization: Partisan divides affecting traditionally non-partisan municipal issues

Effective Advocacy Strategies

Based on our experience and analysis of successful municipal advocacy efforts, we recommend the following strategies for the 89th Legislative Session:

1. Develop Clear Policy Priorities

Effective advocacy begins with well-defined priorities:

  • Focused Agenda: Identify 3-5 top legislative priorities that reflect your community's most critical needs
  • Specific Objectives: Define concrete policy outcomes rather than general positions
  • Consensus Building: Ensure alignment among council members, staff, and community stakeholders
  • Early Adoption: Formally adopt legislative priorities well before the session begins

2. Build and Maintain Relationships

Relationship-driven advocacy is particularly effective in the Texas Legislature:

  • Year-Round Engagement: Maintain regular contact with legislators and staff during the interim
  • Local Connections: Emphasize shared community ties and constituent interests
  • Staff Relationships: Develop strong working relationships with legislative staff who often shape policy details
  • Bipartisan Approach: Cultivate relationships across party lines to ensure broad support

3. Develop Compelling Narratives

Effective communication requires clear, compelling narratives:

  • Community Impact: Frame issues in terms of tangible impacts on residents and businesses
  • Data-Driven Arguments: Support positions with credible data and specific examples
  • Constituent Voices: Incorporate testimonials from affected community members
  • Solutions Focus: Present constructive alternatives rather than simply opposing legislation

4. Form Strategic Coalitions

Collaborative advocacy can amplify municipal influence:

  • Regional Partnerships: Coordinate with neighboring communities on shared priorities
  • Municipal Associations: Engage actively with statewide municipal organizations
  • Cross-Sector Alliances: Partner with business, nonprofit, and community organizations
  • Unusual Allies: Identify non-traditional partners who share specific policy interests

5. Deploy Resources Strategically

Maximize impact through strategic resource allocation:

  • Targeted Presence: Ensure representation at key hearings and meetings
  • Technical Expertise: Provide subject matter experts to inform policy discussions
  • Coordinated Testimony: Prepare diverse voices to testify on priority issues
  • Rapid Response Capacity: Develop systems to quickly analyze and respond to legislative developments

The Power of Personal Engagement

While professional representation is valuable, direct engagement by local elected officials and community leaders often has the greatest impact. Legislators are more responsive to constituents from their districts who can speak authentically about local impacts. A mayor or council member sharing a specific community story can be more persuasive than technical policy arguments.

Tactical Approaches for Key Issues

Different policy issues require tailored advocacy approaches. Here are specific tactics for anticipated priority areas:

Local Revenue and Taxation

  • Fiscal Impact Analysis: Develop detailed analyses showing how revenue constraints affect essential services
  • Taxpayer Value Messaging: Emphasize the connection between local taxes and valued community services
  • Alternative Solutions: Propose balanced approaches that address both taxpayer and service delivery concerns
  • Business Community Allies: Engage local business leaders who understand the connection between municipal services and economic vitality

Infrastructure Funding

  • Project Showcasing: Highlight successful infrastructure projects and unmet needs through site visits and visual materials
  • Economic Impact Data: Quantify the economic benefits of infrastructure investments
  • Matching Fund Strategies: Emphasize local commitment through matching funds and resource leveraging
  • Cross-Jurisdictional Coordination: Demonstrate regional cooperation on infrastructure planning

Land Use and Development

  • Community-Specific Examples: Illustrate how local zoning reflects unique community characteristics and needs
  • Stakeholder Coalitions: Build diverse coalitions including neighborhood groups, developers, and business interests
  • Alternative Approaches: Propose compromise solutions that preserve core municipal authority while addressing specific concerns
  • Implementation Challenges: Highlight practical difficulties with one-size-fits-all approaches

Public Safety

  • Public Safety Professional Voices: Engage police chiefs, fire chiefs, and other public safety leaders in advocacy efforts
  • Community Safety Outcomes: Focus on how local policies contribute to public safety outcomes
  • Operational Expertise: Provide technical input on how proposed legislation would affect day-to-day operations
  • Fiscal Impact Analysis: Quantify the budgetary implications of public safety mandates

Economic Development

  • Success Stories: Showcase economic development wins and their community benefits
  • Competitive Position: Emphasize how incentive tools affect competition with other states
  • Accountability Measures: Highlight existing transparency and performance requirements
  • Business Community Partnerships: Engage economic development partners in advocacy efforts

Practical Implementation Guide

Translating strategy into action requires practical implementation steps:

Pre-Session Preparation (August-December 2024)

  1. Develop Legislative Agenda: Finalize and formally adopt municipal priorities by October 2024
  2. Build Advocacy Team: Identify key staff, elected officials, and community stakeholders for advocacy roles
  3. Prepare Materials: Develop fact sheets, talking points, and supporting data for priority issues
  4. Conduct Pre-Session Meetings: Schedule meetings with legislators and staff before session begins
  5. Join Coalitions: Establish or join coalitions aligned with priority issues

Early Session Engagement (January-February 2025)

  1. Monitor Bill Filings: Establish systems to track and analyze relevant legislation
  2. Conduct Orientation Meetings: Brief legislators and staff on municipal priorities
  3. Identify Committee Targets: Focus engagement on key committees for municipal issues
  4. Prepare Testimony: Identify and prepare potential witnesses for committee hearings
  5. Activate Coalitions: Coordinate advocacy efforts with partner organizations

Mid-Session Advocacy (March-April 2025)

  1. Provide Testimony: Present municipal perspectives at committee hearings
  2. Offer Technical Assistance: Provide drafting suggestions and amendment language
  3. Mobilize Constituent Support: Activate residents and businesses on high-priority issues
  4. Conduct Regular Check-ins: Maintain consistent communication with legislative offices
  5. Adjust Strategies: Adapt approaches based on legislative developments

Late Session Push (May 2025)

  1. Focus on Priorities: Concentrate resources on most critical bills
  2. Conference Committee Engagement: Monitor and influence conference committee negotiations
  3. Prepare Implementation Analysis: Begin analyzing potential impacts of likely-to-pass legislation
  4. Gubernatorial Consideration: Advocate positions during the bill signing period
  5. Document Outcomes: Record successes, challenges, and lessons learned

Post-Session Activities (June-August 2025)

  1. Legislative Recap: Prepare summary of session outcomes for municipal leadership
  2. Implementation Planning: Develop strategies for complying with new requirements
  3. Relationship Maintenance: Thank legislators and staff for their support
  4. Interim Engagement: Participate in interim studies and hearings
  5. Strategy Refinement: Begin planning for the 90th Legislative Session

Communication Best Practices

Effective communication is essential for successful advocacy. Consider these best practices:

Messaging Guidelines

  • Clarity and Brevity: Keep messages clear, concise, and jargon-free
  • Constituent Focus: Frame issues in terms of impacts on the legislator's constituents
  • Solutions Orientation: Offer constructive solutions rather than just identifying problems
  • Consistency: Maintain consistent messaging across different spokespersons and platforms
  • Localization: Tailor messages to reflect specific community contexts and concerns

Communication Channels

  • Direct Engagement: In-person meetings, phone calls, and personalized emails
  • Formal Communications: Official letters, position papers, and testimony
  • Media Relations: Op-eds, editorial board meetings, and press releases
  • Digital Platforms: Municipal websites, social media, and email updates
  • Community Forums: Public meetings, town halls, and community events

Working with the Media

  • Relationship Building: Develop ongoing relationships with key reporters and editors
  • Proactive Outreach: Provide regular updates and background information
  • Rapid Response: Be prepared to respond quickly to relevant news developments
  • Message Discipline: Maintain focus on key points in media interactions
  • Visual Storytelling: Use compelling visuals to illustrate municipal issues

Conclusion

The 89th Texas Legislative Session will present significant challenges and opportunities for municipalities across the state. By developing clear priorities, building strong relationships, crafting compelling narratives, forming strategic coalitions, and deploying resources effectively, municipalities can enhance their influence on legislative outcomes.

Successful advocacy requires a straightforward, relationship-driven approach that emphasizes genuine connections and practical solutions. While the legislative environment may present challenges to local authority, municipalities that engage strategically and proactively can effectively represent their communities' interests.

By beginning preparation now and maintaining consistent engagement throughout the legislative cycle, municipal leaders can position themselves to navigate the 89th Legislative Session successfully and achieve meaningful results for their communities.
