Texas Legislative Calendar: 2025-2026

This comprehensive calendar provides key dates and deadlines for the 89th Texas Legislative Session. Use this resource to plan your government relations strategy and ensure your organization is prepared for critical legislative milestones.

The Texas Legislature meets in regular session every two years, convening on the second Tuesday in January of odd-numbered years. These regular sessions are limited to 140 days. The Governor can also call special sessions as needed, which can last up to 30 days each.

Understanding the Texas Legislative Cycle

The Texas legislative process follows a biennial cycle, with regular sessions occurring in odd-numbered years. Between regular sessions, the interim period serves as a critical time for committee work, study, and preparation for the next session.

During the interim, legislative committees conduct hearings, study assigned topics, and develop recommendations for the upcoming session. This work forms the foundation for many of the bills that will be filed when the Legislature reconvenes.

The most effective government relations strategies account for this full cycle, engaging with lawmakers and staff during both the session and interim periods to build relationships and advance policy priorities.

89th Legislative Session (2025)

89th Legislature Convenes

January 14, 2025
Session

The 89th Texas Legislature convenes at noon for the regular session. The first day includes the election of the Speaker of the House and the adoption of rules for each chamber.

Bill Filing Begins

November 11, 2024
Deadline

First day legislators can pre-file bills for the 89th Legislative Session. Early filing allows bills to receive lower numbers, which can sometimes result in earlier consideration.

60-Day Bill Filing Deadline

March 14, 2025
Deadline

Last day for filing bills and joint resolutions other than local bills, emergency appropriations, and bills that have been declared an emergency by the governor. After this date, a four-fifths vote in the chamber is required to introduce bills.

House Committee Deadline

May 10, 2025
Deadline

Last day for House committees to report House bills and House joint resolutions. This is a critical deadline as bills not reported by this date cannot be considered by the full House.

Last Day of Regular Session

May 26, 2025
Session

Sine die - final day of the 140-day regular session. All legislative business must be concluded by midnight.

Post-Session Deadline

June 22, 2025
Deadline

Last day the governor can sign or veto bills passed during the regular legislative session (20-day period after final adjournment).

Effective Date

September 1, 2025
Deadline

Effective date of bills passed during the regular session (unless specified otherwise). Bills passed with a two-thirds majority in each chamber can take effect immediately upon signing by the governor.

Interim Activities (2025-2026)

Senate Transportation Committee Hearing

June 15, 2025
Hearing

Interim hearing on transportation infrastructure funding and priorities for the next biennium. The committee will hear testimony from state agencies, local governments, and industry stakeholders.

House Energy Resources Committee

July 8, 2025
Hearing

Interim hearing on the state of the Texas energy grid and regulatory considerations. The committee will examine the implementation of legislation passed during the 89th session and consider recommendations for future action.

Interim Charges Released

August 12, 2025
Interim

Lieutenant Governor and Speaker of the House release interim charges for committees to study before the next legislative session. These charges often indicate legislative priorities for the upcoming session.

Senate Health & Human Services Committee

September 23, 2025
Hearing

Interim hearing on healthcare access and affordability in rural Texas communities. The committee will review the implementation of relevant legislation and consider strategies to address ongoing challenges.

House Appropriations Committee

October 14, 2025
Hearing

Interim hearing on state budget priorities and revenue projections for the 2026-2027 biennium. This hearing will begin laying the groundwork for the next state budget.

Pre-Filing for 90th Legislature

November 10, 2026
Deadline

First day legislators can pre-file bills for the 90th Legislative Session (2027).

Important Note

This calendar provides general guidance on key legislative dates. Committee schedules and hearing dates are subject to change. For the most up-to-date information, we recommend checking the official Texas Legislature Online portal at https://capitol.texas.gov/.

Special sessions may be called by the Governor at any time and are not reflected in this calendar. Each special session can last up to 30 days and may only address topics specified by the Governor in the proclamation calling the session.
