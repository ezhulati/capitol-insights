5.3 Economic Development Programs (continued)

  • Bipartisan Messaging: The coalition developed distinct but complementary messaging for different political perspectives, emphasizing:
    • Economic competitiveness for business-oriented legislators
    • Workforce development for education-focused members
    • Revenue growth potential for fiscal conservatives
    • Community benefits agreements for progressive members

Outcome: While a comprehensive Chapter 313 replacement program did not pass, the coalition secured several important victories:

  • Preservation of Chapter 312 municipal tax abatement authority
  • Expansion of eligible uses for economic development sales tax revenue
  • Enhanced tools for industrial development bonds
  • New matching grant program for infrastructure supporting major projects
  • Technical enhancements to Tax Increment Financing authority

Key Lessons:

  1. Competitive Position Data Matters: Comparative data demonstrating Texas' position relative to other states proved particularly persuasive.
  2. Private Sector Partnership Essential: Business community engagement significantly enhanced credibility and influence.
  3. Bipartisan Approach Required: Successful advocacy spanned political divides by addressing diverse priorities.
  4. Incremental Victories Count: Though comprehensive legislation failed, securing multiple smaller enhancements provided meaningful tools.
  5. Long-Term Strategy Needed: Positioning for future legislative sessions began immediately after the 2023 session ended.

6. Conclusion & Action Plan

Effective municipal advocacy in Texas requires strategic planning, coordinated execution, and persistent engagement throughout the legislative cycle. By implementing the strategies outlined in this guide, municipalities can enhance their influence and achieve better policy outcomes.

Key Principles for Success

Based on our analysis and experience, several principles consistently distinguish successful municipal advocacy efforts:

  1. Proactive Engagement: Successful municipalities engage early in the process, shaping the agenda rather than merely reacting to proposals.
  2. Relationship-Based Approach: Building genuine relationships with key decision-makers before specific needs arise creates foundational trust.
  3. Data-Driven Advocacy: Specific, quantifiable information about local impacts is more persuasive than general policy arguments.
  4. Coalition Leadership: Strategic coalitions amplify municipal voices and demonstrate broader support.
  5. Solution Orientation: Proposing alternatives rather than simply opposing problematic legislation enhances credibility and influence.
  6. Consistent Presence: Maintaining visibility and engagement throughout the legislative cycle prevents issues from advancing without municipal input.
  7. Strategic Resource Allocation: Focusing efforts on highest-priority issues and most influential decision points maximizes impact with limited resources.
